The Replica Geometry

To compute $\mathrm{Tr}(\rho_A^n)$, we take $n$ copies of the path integral and glue them cyclically along the interval $A=[u,v]$. This creates an $n$-sheeted Riemann surface $\mathcal{R}_{n,1}$.

Uniformizing Map

The map $w = \left(\frac{z-u}{z-v}\right)^{1/n}$ unwraps the surface onto the complex plane. Each sheet $k=0,\dots,n-1$ is mapped to the angular sector $\frac{2\pi k}{n} \le \arg(w) < \frac{2\pi(k+1)}{n}$. The branch cut $[u,v]$ becomes the positive real axis.
